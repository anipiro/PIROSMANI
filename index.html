The Wanderer of Tbilisi

Niko Pirosmani (1862–1918) was a self-taught painter who roamed the dukhans — the small taverns — of old Tbilisi, painting signboards, feasts, and animals on black oilcloth in exchange for bread, wine, and a roof for the night.

Enter the Gallery

A naive master

He never sold a painting in a gallery. A century later, his works hang in the State Museum of Fine Arts of Georgia and the world calls him a master of Primitivism.

Self-taught

No academy, no master. Pirosmani learned by painting tavern signs and watching the life of old Tiflis pass by his door.

Black oilcloth

His signature canvas. The black ground was never painted over — it became the night, the shadow, the soul of every figure.

A national symbol

Today his face is on the 1-lari banknote and his works are among the most beloved icons of Georgian culture.
